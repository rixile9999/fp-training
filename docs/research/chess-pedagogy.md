# Research: chess-pedagogy

Chess platforms converge on a small set of interlocking mechanics. LESSONS: A chess.com lesson is a short instructional video illustrating one concept, followed by 5-10 on-board challenges where every move (right or wrong) gets focused commentary; lessons sit inside courses inside 4 skill levels (New to Chess/Beginner/Intermediate/Advanced); the first lesson of a course is unlocked, the rest unlock sequentially, and skipping ahead triggers a confirmation prompt. Lichess Learn splits "Chess basics" into stages (one per piece/concept), each with multiple levels; levels are scored with stars based on move efficiency, and exercises are game-like (capture all pieces without hanging your own). Lichess Practice has 4 categories (Checkmates 7 drills, Fundamental Tactics 8, Advanced Tactics 10, Endgames 7) with a global progress percentage; lichess interactive Studies follow a "your turn, find the best move / retry on wrong move" loop. PUZZLES: Every lichess puzzle attempt is literally a Glicko-2 rated game between the user and the puzzle — both carry rating + rating deviation; solving transfers points from puzzle to player, failing the reverse; deviation makes new players/puzzles move fast and stable ones move slowly. A single wrong move anywhere in the multi-move solution fails the whole puzzle; using a hint makes the attempt unrated. Users choose a difficulty band (Easiest→Hardest) defined as an offset (~±300/600) from their own puzzle rating. The puzzle DB was generated by mining 300M analyzed games, re-analyzing candidate positions with Stockfish NNUE at 40 meganodes (>50 CPU-years), human-validating, then auto-tagging ~50+ themes (fork, pin, backRankMate...); pipeline is generator→validator→tagger (github.com/ornicar/lichess-puzzler), and data is published openly. The Puzzle Dashboard shows rating-by-theme, strengths/weaknesses, and lets users replay failed puzzles. TIMED MODES: Puzzle Storm = 3 minutes, 1 point/puzzle, starts ~900-1050 rated and ramps steadily; a combo bar grants time bonuses (5 moves +3s, 12 +5s, 20 +7s, 30 +10s then every 10), a wrong move drains the combo and costs 10s; unrated. Chess.com Puzzle Rush = 3-min, 5-min, or untimed Survival; escalating difficulty; 3 strikes ends the run. Puzzle Streak = no clock, puzzles ordered by ascending rating, one wrong move ends the streak, exactly one skip allowed per run. CHESSABLE MOVETRAINER: SM-2-based spaced repetition where each individual move has its own timer and 8 levels with intervals 4h/1d/3d/1w/2w/1mo/3mo/6mo; any failure resets that move to level 1. "Learning" shows the move + explanation then asks you to reproduce it (wrong → shown answer, quiz restarts; the whole variation is then replayed with emphasis on missed moves); "Review" is pure recall — the line is never shown unless you fail. Options: Key Moves vs All Moves, whole-variation vs randomized review, and cyclical schedules (e.g., Woodpecker 4-week cycles). MISTAKE REVIEW: Lichess "Learn from your mistakes" turns each engine-flagged mistake in your own game into a mini-puzzle (find the better move; cross-checked against the masters DB). Aimchess imports your games, scores 6 skill dimensions against same-rated peers, and builds a Daily Plan of drills (Tactics, Endgame, Defender, 360 Trainer, Blunder Preventer) plus puzzles generated from your own mistakes and "win your lost won-games vs the computer" drills. PEDAGOGY: lessons+puzzles implement immediate corrective feedback (deliberate practice, Ericsson); puzzles/review mode implement retrieval practice (produce the answer, never just recognize); "healthy mix" puzzle selection implements interleaving; Glicko-matched difficulty keeps tasks just above current skill (desirable difficulty); Storm/Rush build speed/automaticity; streaks, XP, combos, leaderboards supply motivation. All of this ports cleanly to Gleam exercises (fill-the-hole, fix-the-compile-error, predict-the-output, write-the-function) rated, tagged, scheduled, and drilled identically.

## Key facts

- chess.com lesson format: one preliminary video illustrating key concepts, then 5-10 interactive board challenges; 'most moves (right or wrong) get focused commentary and advice' (chess.com/lessons).
- chess.com lesson gating: learning path = 4 skill levels (New to Chess, Beginner, Intermediate, Advanced) > courses > lessons; first lesson of each course unlocked, rest unlock sequentially; skipping ahead shows a confirmation prompt; tiles show locked/available/completed state (support.chess.com article 8609703).
- Lichess Learn ('Chess basics') is organized into stages (one per piece/concept, e.g. 'The rook'), each stage containing multiple levels; levels award up to 3 stars based on solving in fewer moves; Combat exercises forbid leaving your own pieces hanging while capturing.
- Lichess Practice section: 4 categories - Checkmates (7 drills), Fundamental Tactics (8), Advanced Tactics (10), Endgames (7) - with a single overall 'Progress: N%' tracker; drills are play-against-engine positions with a goal.
- Lichess interactive Study lessons loop: 'your turn, find the best move for X' -> wrong move shows 'retry' -> author-defined hints and per-move feedback; this is the authoring primitive for community lessons.
- Lichess puzzle ratings: every attempt is a Glicko-2 rated game between user and puzzle; both sides carry rating + rating deviation (RD); solve = user gains / puzzle loses, fail = reverse; high RD (new user or new puzzle) means bigger updates, stable ratings move less.
- Lichess puzzle attempt semantics: a single wrong move anywhere in the multi-move solution fails the entire puzzle (full sequence must be produced); pressing 'hint' makes the attempt unrated.
- Lichess difficulty setting: five bands (Easiest, Easier, Normal, Harder, Hardest) implemented as rating offsets relative to the user's own puzzle rating (Easier is approximately -300; bands span roughly +/-600).
- Lichess puzzle database generation: 300,000,000 analyzed games mined for candidate positions, re-analyzed with Stockfish 12-15 NNUE at 40 meganodes, costing >50 years of CPU time; pipeline = generator (Python, python-chess, finds candidates) -> validator (human review web UI storing quality 0-5 + estimated rating) -> tagger (automatic theme tagging); repo: github.com/ornicar/lichess-puzzler; resulting DB (~3-5M puzzles, CC0) at database.lichess.org.
- Lichess puzzles carry one or more of ~50+ theme tags (fork, pin, skewer, deflection, discoveredAttack, doubleCheck, backRankMate, hangingPiece, attackingF2F7, sacrifice, mateIn1/2/3, endgame phases, etc.); users can train a single theme or a 'healthy mix'.
- Lichess Puzzle Dashboard: rating broken down by theme, explicit strengths/weaknesses lists, solve percentages, filter window up to 90 days, and one-click replay of failed puzzles grouped by theme.
- Puzzle Storm (lichess): 3-minute run, 1 point per solved puzzle, first puzzle ~900-1050 rated, difficulty ramps steadily through the run; combo bar fills per correct MOVE and grants time bonuses at 5 moves +3s, 12 moves +5s, 20 moves +7s, 30 moves +10s then +10s every further 10 moves; a wrong move empties the combo bar AND deducts 10 seconds; player keeps the same color all run; mode is unrated (lichess.org/page/storm).
- Puzzle Rush (chess.com): three formats - 3 minute, 5 minute, and Survival (untimed); all share escalating puzzle difficulty and end after 3 incorrect solutions ('3 strikes'); Survival is pure accuracy/endurance with no clock.
- Puzzle Streak (lichess): no clock; puzzles served in ascending rating order; one wrong move ends the run; exactly one skip allowed per streak (lichess.org/streak).
- Chessable MoveTrainer scheduling: SM-2-derived, 8 levels with review intervals Level1=4h, L2=1d, L3=3d, L4=1w, L5=2w, L6=1mo, L7=3mo, L8=6mo (XP per level 40->100+); a wrong answer resets that item to Level 1; EACH MOVE in a variation has its own independent timer/level (support.chessable.com article 9043598).
- Chessable 'learning' vs 'review': Learn mode shows the move with explanation/arrows then asks you to reproduce it - on failure you are shown the correct move and the quiz restarts, and after finishing a line you replay it from move 1 with emphasis on missed moves; Review mode is pure retrieval - nothing is shown unless you fail a move.
- Chessable configuration options that matter for design: Key Moves (only quiz new moves) vs All Moves (replay whole line from move 1); review 'whole variation' vs 'randomized' ordering; quiz-frequency setting; and cyclical schedules where users set fixed re-solve dates (supports the Woodpecker Method's 4-week first cycle).
- Lichess 'Learn from your mistakes': after requesting computer analysis of your own game, the feature steps through each engine-flagged mistake as a mini-puzzle asking you to find a better move (solution hidden behind a 'view solution' toggle); it cross-checks Stockfish against the masters database so book/master moves are not flagged; board can be flipped to train on the opponent's mistakes.
- Aimchess personalization: imports games from chess.com/lichess, evaluates every move, scores 6 skill dimensions (tactics, endgame, advantage capitalization, resourcefulness, time management, openings) against same-rated peers; serves a Daily Plan of short drills (Tactics, Endgame, Defender, 360 Trainer, Blunder Preventer), puzzles generated from the user's own mistakes, and a drill where you replay games you lost from winning positions against the computer.
- chess.com puzzle ratings use the same Glicko-style logic as game ratings; rating delta depends on user-vs-puzzle rating gap (solve 2500-rated at 2000 = big gain, miss 1000-rated = big loss); using a hint forfeits rating points (only 'progression points' remain, and only if a correct move preceded the hint).
- Pedagogy mapping observed across platforms: immediate corrective per-move feedback (lessons, puzzles) = deliberate practice; forced answer production with no recognition option (puzzles, Chessable Review) = retrieval practice/testing effect; expanding intervals with reset-on-fail (MoveTrainer) = spaced repetition against the forgetting curve; 'healthy mix' random-theme puzzles vs single-theme drills = interleaving vs blocked practice; Glicko-matched difficulty (puzzle ~= user rating) = desirable difficulty/zone of proximal development; Storm/Rush time pressure = automaticity of pattern recognition; streaks/combos/XP/leaderboards = motivation layer, deliberately kept out of the rated-learning loop (Storm is unrated).

## Recommendations

- Lessons -> FP: mirror the chess.com unit: ~1 short text/animation segment per concept (e.g. 'pattern matching on Result') followed by 5-10 tiny in-browser Gleam challenges (fill a hole, fix a compile error, predict output), each giving per-attempt commentary, with sequential unlock plus skip-with-confirmation.
- Puzzles -> FP: build a bank of single-skill exercises ('puzzles') each carrying its own Glicko-2 rating + deviation, updated as a rated game against the user on every first unaided attempt; tag each with themes (recursion, fold, map/filter, Option/Result, pipe operator, tail recursion, type errors, exhaustiveness) so users can drill one theme or a healthy mix; a hint makes the attempt unrated.
- Puzzle generation -> FP: emulate the generator->validator->tagger pipeline: auto-generate candidates (mutate working Gleam code to introduce one bug, or carve holes in known-good functions, verified by compiler + tests as having exactly one intended fix), hand-validate a seed set with estimated ratings, then let user attempts calibrate ratings via Glicko-2.
- Timed mode -> FP: 'Code Storm' = 3 minutes of one-step micro-exercises (choose/complete one expression) starting well below user rating and ramping; combo bar over correct answers granting +3/+5/+7/+10s, wrong answer drains combo and costs 10s; separate 'Streak' mode with no clock, ascending difficulty, one mistake ends it, one skip allowed.
- Spaced repetition -> FP: schedule each micro-skill item (a specific function shape, idiom, or stdlib call) on MoveTrainer-style 8-level intervals (4h/1d/3d/1w/2w/1mo/3mo/6mo), reset to level 1 on failure, one timer per item not per lesson; Learn mode shows the solution then asks the user to retype/reconstruct it, Review mode demands recall cold.
- Mistake review -> FP: log every failed exercise, wrong submission, and compiler-error category; re-serve failures as a personalized retraining queue (lichess 'replay failed puzzles by theme') and compute a per-theme strength/weakness dashboard with a 90-day window; long-term, port Aimchess's idea by analyzing the user's own project code or quiz history for recurring anti-patterns and generating drills from them.
- Sequence the build for a solo dev: (1) lesson engine + exercise runner with per-attempt feedback, (2) tagged puzzle bank with Glicko-2 (the rating math is small and documented in Glickman's paper; lichess's lila implementation is open source), (3) streak/storm modes (pure reuse of the puzzle bank + a timer), (4) SM-2 scheduling (trivial table of intervals), (5) mistake-driven dashboard. Each later feature is a thin layer over the same rated-exercise primitive.

## Risks / limitations

- Exact internal algorithms (lichess Storm's per-puzzle rating ramp, chess.com's puzzle selection, Aimchess's models) are not fully documented publicly; numbers above for Storm bonuses, MoveTrainer intervals, and Rush rules are official, but ramp curves were inferred from forum/user reports - consult lichess-org/lila source for authoritative selection logic.
- Glicko-2 needs a critical mass of attempts per puzzle to converge; with a small initial user base, exercise ratings will be noisy - seed with hand-estimated ratings (as lichess's validator did) and consider higher initial RD.
- Chess puzzles have a single engine-verifiable best line; code exercises often admit many correct solutions, so the 'one wrong move fails the puzzle' mechanic requires constraining exercises (fill one hole, multiple choice, or property-test-checked single-step edits) or accepting test-suite equivalence instead of exact-match.
- SM-2 reset-to-level-1 on failure is known to be harsher than modern FSRS scheduling (one analysis claims FSRS beats SM-2 by 20-30% retention efficiency for chess); SM-2 is simpler to ship but FSRS is an option later.
- Some sources were inaccessible (lichess fandom wiki returned 403; lichess.org/learn is JS-rendered), so stage-by-stage Learn details come from secondary sources.
- Several supporting claims come from forums and third-party reviews rather than official docs; treat fine-grained numbers (e.g. Storm starting range 900-1050, difficulty offsets +/-300/600) as approximate.
